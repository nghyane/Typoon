## Source: Spanish
- Spanish uses gendered nouns and pronouns; the speaker's gender often shows in the verb conjugation and adjective endings — preserve that signal when mapping address forms in the target.
- Tú vs. usted distinction is binding for register: tú → casual/intimate, usted → formal/respectful. Reflect in target's politeness markers.
- Vos (rioplatense) carries regional + casual register. Treat like tú unless the brief notes Argentine/Uruguayan setting.
- Subject pronouns are often dropped — infer subject from verb form and context.
- SFX: Spanish onomatopoeia often differs from English (¡PAM!, ¡ZAS!, ¡CRAC!). Adapt to target conventions.
- Watch for English / non-Latin watermarks bleeding through scanlated raws — those are filtered upstream, but flag if any escape.
