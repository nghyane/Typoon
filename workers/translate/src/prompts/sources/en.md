## Source: English
- English pronouns (I, you, he, she) do NOT encode hierarchy, age, or intimacy.
- Do NOT map English pronouns literally into target-language pronouns.
- Infer speaker-addressee relationship from: titles, vocatives, names, tone, wording, scene role (teacher, boss, officer, parent, classmate, lover, stranger), and visual cues if available.
- Add target-language pronouns or forms of address not explicit in English when needed.
- Rewrite idiomatically; avoid mirroring English sentence structure.
- English idioms, sarcasm, understatement, and terse commands should be translated by meaning and scene intent, not word order.
