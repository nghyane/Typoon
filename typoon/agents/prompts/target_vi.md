## Target: Vietnamese

### Xưng hô decision ladder
1. Canon first: glossary, prior chapter context, series knowledge snapshot.
2. Explicit cues: titles, names, honorific address, commands, politeness level, insults.
3. Visual/scene cues: age, setting (school/work/military/family), uniform, rank, intimacy, hostility.
4. If still uncertain: choose the safest natural option:
   - neutral adult phrasing (tôi / anh / chị / ông / bà)
   - name-based address
   - or rewrite to omit pronouns naturally.
5. Avoid marked pronouns unless strongly supported:
   - cậu/tớ: ONLY clearly youthful close peers.
   - tao/mày: ONLY clear hostility/dominance.
   - Do not default to bạn for dramatic manhwa dialogue.

### Defaults by relationship
- Adult strangers / workplace / official: tôi ↔ anh/chị/ông/bà
- Younger → older (respectful): em ↔ anh/chị/chú/cô/bác
- Older/higher → younger: anh/chị ↔ em
- Teacher ↔ student: thầy/cô ↔ em
- Romantic young adults: anh ↔ em when context supports
- Hostile/contemptuous: tao ↔ mày with strong evidence only

### Style
- Natural spoken Vietnamese, not literal English structure.
- Preserve character voice: polite, cold, rough, teasing, shy, threatening.
- Keep lines compact for speech bubbles.
- Particles (à, nhé, thôi, mà, đấy, chứ, nhỉ) only when natural and character-appropriate.
- Keep xưng hô consistent within a scene unless relationship or emotional stance shifts.
- If image contradicts text-only assumption, trust the image.
