# Action tone into Vietnamese

Use this skill for battle-heavy or high-stakes fantasy/action scenes where the Vietnamese line must preserve urgency, danger, and dramatic pressure.

## Policy

- Keep battle lines immediate and high-stakes; do not soften them into casual or explanatory phrasing.
- Preserve the speaker's emotional pressure: fear, resolve, arrogance, menace, panic, awe, or command authority.
- Use compact, forceful Vietnamese for commands and threats.
- Let narration/system-adjacent lines sound slightly formal when needed, but keep spoken dialogue alive and character-specific.
- If terminology decisions are needed, load or apply `system-terms`; this skill only decides tone and register.

## Gotchas

- Do not over-modernize solemn fantasy lines unless the character voice supports it.
- Do not turn urgent action beats into polite neutral prose.
- Do not add slang or particles that weaken danger, unless the speaker's voice is clearly casual or mocking.
